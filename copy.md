# Xudumela — Copy: original vs rewritten

Two versions for every key block of site copy. **Original** is verbatim from the live site. **Rewritten** is what the rebuild should ship with, pulled tighter, more specific, and benchmarked against the competitor field. Hyphens used in place of em-dashes throughout. No "meaningful", no "authentic", no "journey", no AI-cadence stock phrases.

## Home — hero

**Original**
> Welcome to Xudumela
> Where Conservation meets Wilderness
> More than a place to stay, Xudumela is a living conservancy shaped by wildlife, people, education, and a deep connection to the transitional landscapes surrounding the Okavango Delta.

**Rewritten**
> A working conservancy on the Xudum Channel, 20 minutes from Maun.
> Sleep in a safari tent or pitch your own. Walk with trackers. Pole a mokoro at sunset. Every night funds the Connect Trust.

Why it's tighter: it names the river, names the distance, names the activities, names the trust. The original has none of that.

> Xudumela Conservancy and Camp Grounds was created from a vision to protect and celebrate the important landscapes surrounding the Okavango Delta near Maun. What began as a simple idea has evolved into a growing conservancy welcoming travellers, schools, events, and conservation partnerships - creating a place where people can reconnect with nature in a way that feels personal, grounded, and real.

**Rewritten**
> Xudumela is a privately-run conservancy on the southern edge of the Okavango Delta. We started small and we plan to stay that way. The land is here to be protected first and shared second.
>
> Guests come for the Delta. Schools come for the field-study programmes. Researchers come because we keep the camp footprint light enough to leave the land alone. And in October we host Poachella, a 400-person boutique festival raising funds for anti-poaching and community work through the Connect Trust.

## Home — Essentials grid (8 cards)

**Original** mostly reads like padded captions. Examples:

> Airport Transfers: Seamless transfers connecting guests between Maun Airport, Xudumela, and surrounding wilderness experiences.
> Swimming Pool: Cool off beside the water while wildlife and wilderness rhythms unfold around you naturally.

**Rewritten** - factual, short, no filler

- **Airport transfers** — Pickup from Maun Airport. 25 minutes by road, prebookable.
- **Wifi** — Reliable in the main area. Patchy at the tents, by design.
- **Meals** — Breakfast included on tents. Full board on request. Campfire dinners on Friday and Saturday nights.
- **Housekeeping** — Daily refresh on tents. Camp sites self-service.
- **Laundry** — Same-day service. Pula 80 per load.
- **Pool** — Spring-fed plunge pool by the main deck.
- **Activities** — Bushmen archery, walking, tracking, mokoro, birding, elephant hide.
- **Conservation** — Every booking includes a $15/night conservation levy paid to the Connect Trust.

## Voice rules used for this rewrite

- No em-dashes (only regular hyphens)
- No "meaningful", "authentic", "journey", "embark", "unlock"
- No "not just X but Y"
- Numbers wherever a number exists
- One claim per sentence
- Address the reader as you, never as "guests" in third person
- The conservancy is "we" or "Xudumela", never "the team behind Xudumela"
- Currency stays consistent within a block: USD for room rates (matches what international guests expect to compare), Pula for local fees and the festival (matches Quicket)
